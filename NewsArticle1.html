Ino8 Robotics Pvt

Under the guidance of the Inventors Commission of Sri Lanka, Ino8 Robotics Pvt. The Department of Wildlife Conservation has agreed to purchase 50 locally made collars for wild elephants for the year 2021, an invention that seeks to secure their intellectual property rights. The project, which is valued at over Rs. 22 million, is expected to save a large amount of money flowing abroad, and the technology, which is more advanced than the collars currently in use, will be available at a lower cost. Special mention should be made of the invaluable support received from the Department of Wildlife Conservation in this regard, which is a significant achievement in the operation of the Commission to Empower the Sri Lankan Economy through local innovations

This invention is a joint venture between the Inventors' Commission of Sri Lanka and the Department of Wildlife Conservation, a project that combines human-wildlife conflict with innovative solutions, minimizing conflicts between wildlife and humans, which is a current social need, as well as human resources, wildlife and agriculture.

Our sense is that protection can be activated as a turning point in today's society. The dedication of these inventors, who worked tirelessly day and night to improve their inventions as needed, is a great example to all other inventors. Implemented by the Inventors Commission of Sri Lanka in collaboration with the Department of Wildlife Conservation, this program is supported by the State Ministry of Skills Development, Vocational Education, Research and Innovation and the State Ministry of Wildlife Conservation, Elephant Fences and Trenches, Ministry of Forest Replanting and Forest Resources Development. Full contribution received.

Skills Development, Vocational Education, Research and Innovation Hon. Minister of State Sita Arambepola and other Hon. We should also recall the unconditional support received from State Minister Wimalaweera Dissanayake.
