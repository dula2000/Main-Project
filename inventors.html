Inventions Support In Sri Lanka


Sri Lanka Inventors Commission (SLIC)

Sri Lanka Inventors Commission (SLIC) is a statutory body established in terms of the Sri Lanka Inventors Incentives Act No. 53 of 1979. The principal objective of setting up of the SLIC is to promote inventiveness of the nation by providing technical, financial and legal assistance. The Commission consists of a Commissioner and two Assistant Commissioners. The members of the Commission are appointed by the Cabinet of Ministers for a period of 03 years on the recommendation made by the line Minister. Since 2010 SLIC functions under the Ministry of Technology and Research.

Promoting and facilitating for nations innovations are the main responsibilities of Sri Lanka Inventors Commission (SLIC). Commission conducts several programmes with the intention of promoting innovative culture within the future generation.Commission has identified that the students of schools, universities and tertiary education institutions are playing a vital role in the process of promoting innovative culture throughout the Sri Lanka. SLIC has initiate a programme to reach the students of schools, universities and tertiary education institutions by establishing young inventors clubs within their education institutions. SLIC has established more than 200 no of inventors clubs by conducting relevant awareness programmes throughout Sri Lanka. Special opportunities such as awareness on inventions and patents are also benefited to the young inventors of schools, universities and tertiary education institutions. One of the main strategies adopted by the commission to assist inventors commercialize their products is by building partnerships with various private sector organizations in addition to providing them with stalls at the national exhibition.

National Intellectual Property Office (NIPO)


The National Intellectual Property Office of Sri Lanka, established under the Intellectual Property Act, No. 36 of 2003 is mandated with the administration of the Intellectual Property System in Sri Lanka. It was first established on January 1, 1982 with the same mandate under the provisions of the Code of Intellectual Property Act, No. 52 of 1979, which was repealed in 2003. vision of NIPO, To be a leading government institution providing quality focused productive services with an objective to achieve a market oriented intellectual property system for the country. Mission of NIPO, Ensuring an intellectual property system which promotes economic sustainability through Intellectual Property creation, protection and commercialization.

You can get the following functions from National Intellectual Property Office.

  • Administration of Intellectual Property including registration of trademarks, patents, industrial designs and registration and administration of collective management societies.
  • Collection and dissemination of intellectual property information.
  • Building awareness on the subject of intellectual property.
  • Promotion of the use of intellectual property in the economic development process.
  • Fulfillment of the international obligations of Sri Lanka relating to Intellectual property and promotion of international and regional co-operation on intellectual property.
  • Proposing policies with respect to intellectual property.
  • Dispute resolution in the field of copyright and related rights, facilitate the enforcement of intellectual property rights.
  • Registration and administration of Intellectual Property Agents.

Coordinating Secretariat for Science, Technology and Innovation (COSTI)


The Coordinating Secretariat for Science, Technology and Innovation (COSTI) was established on February 1, 2013 as mandated by the cabinet decision of September 9, 2011 with the specific aim of coordination and monitoring of Science, Technology and Innovation activities in the country. It will also work towards promoting value addition and commercialization in line with the National Science Technology and Innovation (STI) Strategy of Sri Lanka approved by the Cabinet in August 2010.

Mission of to establish a world class National Research and Innovation Eco-system to generate strategic and sustainable technologies and innovations to win the "economic war" by:

  • on areas of core competencies and resource linked opportunities;
  • Preparing our people for a knowledge society through improved scientific literacy; and
  • Upholding sustainable principles in all spheres of activity.

Technological revolutions have been the basis for economic development in the world. It is envisaged that Science and Technology based innovation is crucial to achieve sustainable development in the country in the form of rapid economic development, social justice and environmental quality. Conversely, hi-tech industries in Sri Lanka still remains at a primitive stage which requires intensive novel thinking towards S&T Innovation based Industry via promoting value addition and commercialization. At a time where technological transformations continuing at a rapid pace and the emergence of a globalized market place, all countries are heading towards becoming more innovative. In this backdrop to escalate our GDP throughincrease of high-tech value added exports COSTI had recognized emerging technologies such as biotechnology, nanotechnology, advanced materials, electronics &robotics, advanced design &manufacturing and ICT & space technologyas the model tool to embrace and foster innovation for Sri Lanka.

Arthur C Clarke Institute for Modern Technologies (A C C I M T)


Arthur C. Clarke Institute for Modern Technologies is an institute for research and technology transfer in Sri Lanka. It is named after its founder patron, Sir Arthur C. Clarke, the famous British science fiction author, inventor and futurist. Vision of Arthur C. Clarke Institute, To be a leading innovation centre for Modern Technologies in the region. Mission of Arthur C. Clarke Institute To develop, foster and facilitate the domestic base of modern technological capabilities through innovation, R & D, training, industrial services and international collaboration”


You can get the following functions from Arthur C. Clarke Institute.
To accelerate the introduction of modern technologies to Sri Lanka by
1)initiating, promoting and conducting research and development in the application of modern technologies
2)providing research and development support to the Government and private sector undertakings in the application of modern technologies and
3)training of personnel in modern technologies to meet the needs of the Government and private sector undertakings and
To promote future studies
1)The areas of modern technologies include Communication and related Sciences, Information Technology, Electronics, Micro-electronics, Space Technologies, Robotics, Photonics and new materials.
