National Innovation Mission - 2021 National Science Day is celebrated with the theme of reviving dreams ...


  The National Science Day Celebration organized by the State Ministry of Skills Development, Vocational Education, Research and Innovation under the theme “National Innovation Mission - Presenting Dreams” was held under the patronage of President Gotabhaya Rajapaksa at Temple Trees recently (10). Held. The highlight of the event was the launch of the National Innovation Mission, the presentation of the Dasis Awards to the Excellence of the Millennium Innovation - 2020 Innovation Competition and the launch of the new Oxygen Concentrator, a groundbreakingly significant new invention from Nard. It also marked the beginning of the National Science Week, which coincides with National Science Day and runs from November 10-16.

The National Innovation Mission (NIM) launched the National Innovation Mission (NIM) as a step towards achieving the goal of making Sri Lanka a hub of innovation through a 'Vision Prosperity' technology based society in line with the National Policy Framework ', which specializes in science and technology.

Implemented under the five main themes of Environmental Innovation, Social Innovation, Service Innovation and Security Innovation. This is a national program for innovators. On the occasion of National Science Day, the President presented financial assistance and equipment to nine selected schools at the provincial level, symbolizing the establishment of a network of innovative laboratories for school children under this operation. The objectives of celebrating National Science Day and the corresponding National Science Week are, Educating the public on the role of science and technology in creating an informed society that can achieve the Sustainable Development Goals. Promoting national and international collaborations for the application of science and technology in the field of social development. Contribute to the field of science and technology to eradicate poverty. Interventions in the field of science and technology and public awareness on national development. During the National Science Week, a number of programs aimed at advancing the field of science, technology and innovation are implemented under the guidance of the Ministry and its affiliates. National Science Week is one day of the week dedicated to one particular theme. Accordingly, the week of November 10 - 16 has been designated as National Science Day, Innovation Day, Space Technology Day, Patent Day, Energy Day, Innovation Promotion Day and Science Promotion Day respectively. On those days, there will be a major theme program and concurrent programs.

Namal Rajapaksa, Minister of Sports and Youth Affairs, Dr. Sita Arambepola, Specialist Minister of State for Skills Development, Vocational Education, Research and Innovation, Vijitha Berugoda, Minister of State, Prof. Kapila Perera, Secretary to the Ministry of Education, Skills Development, Vocational Education, Research and Innovation Secretary to the Ministry of State Deepa Liyanage, Foreign Ambassadors, Scientists, Government Officials, School Children and many others were present
